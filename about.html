About From Grass to Grace: The Musical Journey

Biography

Durk Derrick Banks was born on October 19, 1992, in the Englewood neighborhood on the South Side of Chicago, Illinois. He grew up with a great deal of responsibility at home, as his father was incarcerated when he was seven months old. He recalled that there were times when he did not have enough food at home when he was younger. He began to establish a presence on social media channels like Myspace and YouTube; He grew fond of the idea of being a rapper as his online fan base started to expand.

Banks took his career more seriously after becoming a father at 19 and dropping out of school at Paul Robeson High School to join the Black Disciples, a street gang in Chicago. Soon after joining, he began to get into trouble with the law and served time in October 2011 due to gun charges, including possession of a firearm with a defaced serial number.

Despite displaying a very bad image to society at large, Durk was able to rise from adversity and start releasing music in 2012, where he gained huge recognition; of course, not immediately but after dropping a couple of singles.

Contributions and Achievements

  1. 2013–2015: Signing to OTF and Def Jam Recordings and Signed to the Streets 1 and 2
  2. 2015–2016: Remember My Name and Lil Durk 2X
  3. 2017–2019: Mixtapes, new label and albums; Signed to the Streets 3, Just Cause Y'all Waited
  4. 2021: Won NAACP awards for the best Outstanding HipHop/Rap Song with Drake
  5. 2021-2022: Nominated thrice for the Grammy Awards
  6. 2020–2022: Just Cause Y'all Waited 2, The Voice, and 7220
  7. 2023–present: Almost Healed
  8. 2024: Could be nominated for the best melodic rap performance with J Cole
  9. 2023-present: Top 15 Forbes Top Earning Artist
